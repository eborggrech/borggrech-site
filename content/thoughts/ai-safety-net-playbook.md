## What this playbook is for
 
For teams adopting AI quickly and feeling two fears at once:
- “We’re moving too slow if we don’t use it.”
- “We’re taking risks we can’t see.”
 
This playbook is designed for small teams who need speed *and* reliability.
 
## Outcomes you should expect
 
- Clear boundaries on where AI is allowed.
- Human accountability remains visible.
- Fewer quality incidents caused by unchecked output.
- Faster adoption because people feel safe using it.
 
## The operating principle
 
AI does not remove accountability.
It changes what the human is accountable for.
 
Guardrails are not heavy policy.
Guardrails are design constraints:
- where AI can be used
- what must be verified
- who owns the outcome
- how incidents are handled
 
## The artifacts (copy/paste)
 
**AI Use Boundaries (one page)**
- Allowed workflows
- Not allowed (sensitive data, final approvals, legal commitments)
- Required verification steps
 
**Human-in-the-loop Checklist**
- What must be checked (facts, numbers, tone, commitments)
- What must be cited (sources, assumptions)
 
**AI Incident Note** (one page)
- What happened | Impact | Root cause | Guardrail update | Owner
 
**Prompt + Output Log** (optional)
- Not for surveillance; for learning and repeatability
 
## Step-by-step implementation
 
1) Pick 3 workflows where AI helps but risk is manageable.
2) Define verification: what must the human check before output ships?
3) Assign ownership: one person owns the final outcome.
4) Define “no-go zones” (data and decisions that must remain human).
5) Create an incident path: when output causes harm, document and update guardrails.
6) Review monthly: expand allowed workflows only when reliability is proven.
 
## Cadence (how you keep it alive)
 
Weekly:
- Quick review: where did AI help, where did it create risk?
 
Monthly:
- Update allowed workflows.
- Review AI incidents and adjust verification.
- Refresh boundaries as the business changes.
 
## Metrics that prove it’s working
 
- Number of AI-assisted outputs shipped.
- Quality incidents attributable to AI output.
- Time saved in selected workflows.
- Adoption rate (how many people use it confidently).
- Rework rate (output revised after shipping).
 
## Common failure modes (and fixes)
 
- **Over-restriction kills adoption.** Fix: allow low-risk workflows first.
- **No verification leads to incidents.** Fix: simple checklist tied to workflow.
- **No owner means blame storms.** Fix: one DRI per output type.
- **Silent failures repeat.** Fix: one-page incident notes + guardrail updates.
 
## Example: what this looks like in a real week
 
A team allowed AI for:
- drafting customer emails (human reviews before send)
- summarizing meetings into decisions (decision log required)
- first-pass SOP drafts (owner approves)
 
They banned AI for:
- legal commitments
- pricing promises
- sensitive personal data
 
Incidents dropped because verification was explicit, not assumed.
 
## Quick start checklist
 
- Choose 3 allowed workflows.
- Write a one-page boundary doc.
- Add a verification checklist.
- Assign a DRI for each output type.
- Start logging incidents when they happen, without blame.
